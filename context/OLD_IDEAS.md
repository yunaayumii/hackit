# Old Ideas and Why They Are Not Used

These are earlier directions that are either too broad, too common, or too risky for the hackathon.

## Generic Budgeting App

Why not used:

- Too common.
- Weak fintech differentiation.
- Hard to make judges care in a 5-minute pitch.
- Does not directly address a high-stakes financial decision.

## Generic Loan Comparison App

Why not used:

- Already exists in the Philippines through platforms like Digido, Moneezy, LoanOnline, and Loanly.
- Often optimized around finding or applying for loans, not deciding whether borrowing is wise.
- Easy for judges to dismiss as another marketplace.

## Pure Scam Detector

Why not used:

- Too narrow if the user's loan provider is legitimate.
- Risky to overpromise because scam detection can never be perfect.
- Can become more of a cybersecurity tool than a fintech-for-change solution.
- Better as a support feature, not the main product.

## AI Credit Scoring Tool

Why not used:

- Legally and ethically messy.
- Could imply lending decisions, discrimination risk, or regulatory concerns.
- Requires data that a hackathon prototype probably should not collect.
- Hard to defend without real validation.

## Full Sari-Sari Store Management App

Why not used:

- Existing players already cover inventory, ordering, bookkeeping, and supply chain.
- Too large for a hackathon prototype.
- The fintech angle becomes diluted unless the focus is borrowing or cash flow.

## Digital Wallet or Payment App

Why not used:

- Digital payments are already mainstream and competitive.
- Requires integrations, compliance, trust, and security that are unrealistic for the hackathon.
- Does not target the strongest observed gap, which is financial resilience and decision support.

## Final Direction Kept

KitaGuard: a borrowing readiness checker for small informal sellers.

Why kept:

- Specific user.
- Specific financial decision.
- Clear demo.
- Plausible prototype.
- Uses recent Philippine data.
- Avoids pretending to be a lender, bank, wallet, or credit bureau.
