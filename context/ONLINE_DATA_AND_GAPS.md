# Online Data and Gaps

Use this as the evidence base for fintech concept work. The goal is not to collect random statistics; the goal is to justify a narrow product decision.

## Key Data Points

- Digital payments are already mainstream in the Philippines. BSP reported that digital transactions reached 57.4% of monthly retail payment volume and 59.0% of value in 2024.
- Merchant payments are a major driver of digital payment growth. This matters because small sellers are already part of the digital payment environment.
- Financial inclusion is still uneven. BSP's 2025 Consumer Finance and Inclusion Survey reported that only 50% of Filipino adults owned a formal financial account, down from 56% in 2021.
- E-money account ownership stayed at 36%, while bank account ownership was 23%. This suggests many people may transact digitally without deeper access to formal financial products.
- Mobile-first is realistic. BSP's 2025 CFIS overview reported that 90% of adults have mobile phones and 89% use the internet.
- Financial resilience is weak. BSP reported that only about 3 in 10 Filipino adults said their current finances or savings would last through a shock.
- Scam and fraud risk is rising around digital finance. CICC reported 10,004 cybercrime complaints in 2024, more than triple 2023, with almost PHP198 million in reported losses.

## Product Implications

- Building another wallet or payment app is a weak direction. Adoption is not the main gap.
- Building a generic loan comparison app is also weak. Several services already compare loan offers, rates, and terms.
- The stronger gap is decision support: helping users decide whether a financial action is safe for their actual cash flow.
- For informal earners and small sellers, a legal loan can still be personally unsafe if repayment timing breaks inventory, food, rent, transport, or school cash flow.
- Scam detection should be a secondary feature. It is useful, but too narrow and risky if treated as the main promise.

## Observed Market Similarities

- Loan comparison platforms exist, such as Digido, Moneezy, LoanOnline, and Loanly. They focus on finding or applying for loans.
- Financial calculator tools exist, such as Savvy Spender. They help with affordability, loan comparison, debt planning, and other calculations.
- Sari-sari store platforms exist, such as Sari.PH, GrowSari, and SariPondo. They focus on store management, ordering, inventory, bookkeeping, supply chain, or inventory credit.
- Loan readiness systems exist internationally, such as Parlay, but they are more formal SMB/lender-side infrastructure.

## Gap Worth Targeting

The gap is not "people cannot find loans."

The gap is:

> Informal Filipino sellers need a simple way to check whether a loan fits their real cash cycle before they borrow.

This is narrower than generic financial literacy and more useful than a static calculator.
