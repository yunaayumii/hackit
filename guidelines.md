# UP Hack-It-UP 2026 Onboarding Document

## ABOUT TECH WEEK 2026: WRECK-IT, FIX-IT
As we enter a new chapter, technology continues to evolve and achieve new heights. Wreck-It, Fix-It aims to bring together broad-minded enthusiasts from different fields to learn, be challenged, and keep up with the ongoing evolution of today's technological advancements.

## ABOUT UP SOCOMSCI
Celebrating its 30th year, **UP Society of Computer Scientists (UP SoComSci)** is a non-profit, non-stock student organization committed to promoting greater awareness of computer technology and its wider application beneficial to the Filipino society. Through various annual events like webinars, career talks, workshops, and others, students and professionals alike get exposed to numerous opportunities in the field of Computer Science.

## ABOUT HACK IT UP!
**Hack-it-UP** is UP SoComSci's annual coding competition that gathers students from different universities across the country. Teams of exactly 4 members are challenged to develop innovative, technology-driven solutions for real-world issues tied to specific Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), all within a high-pressure, limited timeframe.

## THEME
This year's general theme is: **FinTech for Change**

Please note that this is only the general theme. A more specific theme, along with detailed requirements and judging criteria, will be officially revealed during the Onboarding session on May 12, 2026.

Teams will be working within the space of Financial Technology (FinTech) and its potential for meaningful, real-world impact in the Philippine context. The full scope and direction of the theme will be announced at Onboarding. Attendance is required, and the specific SDGs will be revealed during the onboarding.

## ELIGIBILITY AND REGISTRATION

### Who Can Join
* Hack-It-UP 2026 is open to all currently enrolled college students from any university in the Philippines.
* Each team must consist of exactly 4 members.
* Members from different universities may form one team.
* A maximum of 25 teams will be accepted. Slots are filled on a first-come, first-served basis upon completion of registration and payment.

### Registration Fee
* **P200.00 per team.**
* Payment details will be provided upon registration.
* Registration is only confirmed upon receipt of payment.
* Only the first 25 teams to complete registration and payment will be accepted.

## GENERAL GUIDELINES

### Preliminary Round (Eliminations)
All registered teams must submit their initial project idea on or before the submission deadline. A panel of evaluators will assess all submissions based on the Preliminary Judging Criteria. The top 7 teams with the highest scores will advance to the Finals.

### Video Guidelines
* Length: Maximum of 3 minutes.
* Format: .mp4
* Content:
    * A short discussion of the problem and your proposed FinTech solution.
    * Must include a voice-over from at least one team member.
    * Must not include any element that reveals the identity of your school.
    * Be creative and make it compelling.
    * File name format: `<TeamName>_<ProjectTitle>`

### Paper Guidelines
* Length: 1-page summary only.
* Format: .pdf
* Layout: Times New Roman, 12 pt, single-spaced, 1-inch margins.
* Content:
    * Abstract
    * Short description of the problem
    * Objectives
    * Chosen SDG and its relevance to the proposed solution
    * Must not include any element that reveals the identity of your school.
* File name format: `<TeamName>_<ProjectTitle>`

### How to Submit
1.  Create a folder in Google Drive.
2.  Rename the folder to your team name.
3.  Upload both your video (.mp4) and paper (.pdf) inside the folder, following the file name format above.
4.  Click Share on the folder.
5.  Add socomsci.upm@gmail.com as a Viewer. Make sure to tick the "Notify people" checkbox.
6.  A confirmation email will be sent once your submission has been reviewed.

## PRELIMINARY JUDGING CRITERIA
All submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges based on the following criteria:

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Innovation / Originality & Creativity | 30% | How novel, original, and creative is the proposed FinTech solution? Does it offer a fresh approach to the problem? |
| Idea Presentation | 30% | How clearly and effectively is the idea communicated in the video and paper? Is the proposed solution easy to understand and well-articulated? |
| Feasibility | 20% | Is the proposed solution realistic and implementable? Does it consider practical constraints such as resources, technology, and time? |
| Technology Integration / Adoption | 20% | How effectively does the solution leverage appropriate technology? Is the tech choice well-suited to the problem and SDG addressed? |

## EXPECTED OUTPUT
Teams are expected to develop and present a technology-driven FinTech solution that directly addresses a real-world financial problem in the Philippine context. The output does not need to be a fully deployed product, but it must demonstrate a clear, well-thought-out concept backed by appropriate technology.

At minimum, teams should be able to present the following:
* **A clearly defined problem:** A specific, real-world financial issue that the solution aims to solve, with a clear connection to the chosen SDG.
* **A proposed tech-driven solution:** A concrete idea for a technology-based product or system that addresses the problem. This may include but is not limited to: a mobile application, a web platform, an AI/ML-powered tool, a blockchain-based system, or a data analytics solution.
* **A working prototype or demo (for Finals):** Finalist teams are expected to present a working prototype, mockup, or functional demo of their solution during Demo Day. This does not need to be production-ready, but it must be demonstrable and interactive enough to illustrate how the solution works.
* **A clear articulation of impact:** Teams must be able to explain who benefits from their solution, how it creates impact, and why it is a better approach than existing alternatives.
* **A feasible implementation plan:** Teams should outline how their solution could realistically be built and deployed, including the technologies to be used, target users, and potential challenges.

## TEAM WORKING FOCUS FOR THIS PROJECT

For this workspace, treat Hack-It-UP 2026 as a fintech-for-impact build sprint. Project work should stay anchored to the official brief above while moving toward a proposal and prototype that judges can understand quickly.

Strong candidate ideas should be specific about:
* **Target users:** For example, underbanked Filipino households, students, gig workers, OFWs and their families, sari-sari store owners, microentrepreneurs, cooperatives, or first-time digital finance users.
* **Financial problem:** For example, budgeting, savings, remittances, access to responsible credit, cash-flow tracking, fraud awareness, financial literacy, payment friction, or microinsurance discovery.
* **Technology approach:** For example, a mobile-first web app, AI-assisted financial coach, transparent credit-readiness tool, fraud-risk checker, receipt or expense tracker, community savings dashboard, or analytics-driven recommendation system.
* **SDG link:** The solution should clearly connect to SDG 1, SDG 8, or SDG 10 through a realistic impact path.
* **Prototype path:** The demo should show a working flow, not only static slides. Simulated data is acceptable when real financial integrations are impractical or unsafe.

Teams should be careful with concepts that require live bank integrations, payment processing, production KYC, regulated lending decisions, or sensitive personal financial data. If these appear in the idea, the prototype should clearly simulate them and explain the real-world requirements separately.
